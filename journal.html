When Nature Answered

On the fennel plant, the silence after intwaso, and what it means to remember.

I. The day nature answered me

I used to dream.

Not the kind of dreams you forget when you wake up — but the kind that stay. The kind that speak. That guide. That reveal what is coming before it arrives. My dreams have always been a part of me. They were never random. They were messages.

And then, something changed.

I still dreamed after intwaso. But I could not remember. It was like waking up with something missing — like someone had taken a language I had always understood and removed it from me overnight. Quietly. Without explanation.

I didn't understand it. I didn't accept it either.

So I did what I've always done. I spoke. Not through ritual. Not through ceremony. Not through anything I was taught to use. I sat with myself, in silence, and I spoke out loud — because I believe we are never alone. I believe we are heard.

"I need help remembering my dreams."

I said it more than once. Not perfectly. Not powerfully. Just honestly.

Then one morning, on my way to work, I saw it.

A plant. Standing alone, just above my yard, along the path I walk every single day. I cannot explain why I had never noticed it before. It was green in a way that felt… alive. Not just alive — but present. Like it had been waiting.

It pulled me toward it.

The first day, I looked. The second day, I noticed. The third day, I listened.

I took it with me and asked the people around me what it was. The answers were different. One said it helps with remembering dreams. Another said it is used for cleansing — to clear out idliso. I didn't know what to believe. So I left it there.

But later that day, something happened that I still cannot fully explain.

I opened TikTok. The very first post I saw… was that plant. No search. No picture. No algorithm I had fed. There it was.

Fennel.

Used for dream recall.

I just stared at the screen. Because in that moment, something inside me shifted. Not slowly. Not gently. Completely.

That was the moment I understood — nature speaks. Not loudly. Not in words. But it responds. It listens. It aligns. It answers in ways that cannot always be explained, only experienced.

That plant did not grow there by accident. It answered me. And from that moment, I started paying attention.

To what I say. To what I feel. To what surrounds me.

Because everything is connected. And sometimes, the answer is already growing right in front of you.

II. The silence after intwaso

There is a version of me that existed before intwaso. I think about her sometimes.

She was open in a way that felt natural. She didn't question her connection to her dreams. She didn't try to understand everything — she simply experienced. And then I went through something that was supposed to bring me closer. And in many ways, it did. But I also lost things I never thought I could lose.

My dreams stopped speaking to me the way they used to.

And no one talks about that part.

No one talks about what it feels like to gain knowledge but lose a language your soul has always known. No one talks about the quiet confusion. The way you sit with yourself and ask: what changed in me?

For a long time, I thought something was wrong with me.

Transformation is not always gentle.
Sometimes you don't just grow — you shed.
And sometimes, what you shed feels like a loss
before it feels like growth.

Maybe I didn't lose my dreams. Maybe I lost the version of myself that knew how to receive them. And now I am learning again — not from people, not from systems — but from myself. From silence. From nature. From remembering who I was before everything told me who I should be.

This is the part no one prepares you for. The in-between. The space where you are no longer who you were, but not yet who you are becoming.

And maybe that is exactly where the real awakening begins.

III. I AM NATURE

I am learning to listen without needing words. To notice without forcing meaning. To trust that what finds me is not random.

A plant grows where I walk. A thought arrives when I go quiet. A feeling rises when I stop resisting.

Nothing is random.

I am not separate from what surrounds me. I am part of it. I am the question — and I am the answer, finding its way back to me.

I am nature.
And nature… remembers.
