Method

Rice Water Strength Rinse

Uncooked Rice & Water

Traditionally used across Asia and West Africa to support stronger, softer, and shinier hair. Simple. Free. Effective.

Ingredients
  • ½ cup uncooked rice
  • 2 cups water
Method
  1. Rinse rice briefly to remove dirt or residue.
  2. Place rice into a bowl with the water and soak for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the water into a clean container — this is your rinse.
  4. After shampooing, pour rice water over the hair and scalp.
  5. Leave for 10–15 minutes before rinsing thoroughly.

Free Recipe

Sauerkraut

Fermented Cabbage & Carrot

One of the oldest gut medicines on earth. Made from two ingredients, no heat, no equipment — just salt, time, and patience.

Ingredients
  • 1 medium cabbage, finely sliced
  • 1–2 carrots, grated
  • 1–2 tbsp natural salt (sea, Celtic, or Himalayan)
Method
  1. Place cabbage and carrots into a large bowl.
  2. Add salt and massage thoroughly with clean hands until the vegetables begin releasing liquid.
  3. Continue massaging until enough natural brine forms at the bottom of the bowl.
  4. Pack tightly into a clean glass jar, pressing firmly so the vegetables remain submerged beneath the liquid.
  5. Leave a small space at the top of the jar for expansion.
  6. Cover loosely and leave at room temperature for 3–7 days, depending on temperature and desired sourness.
  7. Once ready, store in the fridge.
Start with 1 tablespoon daily and increase gradually as the body adjusts. The gut knows what it needs — follow its lead.

The Evening Surrender

Chamomile, Mint & Honey Tea

The day asks everything of us. This tea asks nothing. It simply holds the space between what has been and what will come.

Ingredients
  • 2 tsp dried chamomile flowers (or 1 tea bag)
  • 4–5 fresh peppermint leaves (or ½ tsp dried)
  • 350ml just-boiled water
  • 1 tsp raw honey
  • Thin slice of fresh ginger (optional)
Method
  1. Place chamomile, mint, and ginger in a cup.
  2. Pour water just off the boil — not at a full rolling boil.
  3. Cover and steep for 7 minutes to keep volatile oils in.
  4. Strain. Add honey only after straining.
  5. Hold the cup with both hands. Drink 30 minutes before bed.
Chamomile doesn’t force sleep. It simply creates the conditions for it — and that is enough.

A Note on Water

Not everyone has access to filtered or bottled water. And even when water is available, it doesn’t always feel or taste clean.

If filtered water is not accessible to you, do what you can. Boiling water is one of the simplest and most effective ways to make it safer. Let it cool before use.

There are also traditional methods that have been used for generations. One of them uses Moringa seeds.

Natural Water Support — Moringa Seeds

The seeds of the Moringa tree have been used in different parts of the world to help clarify water. When crushed and added to water, they bind to particles, helping dirt and impurities settle at the bottom.

  1. Crush 1–2 dried Moringa seeds into a fine powder.
  2. Add to 1 litre of water.
  3. Stir well for a few minutes.
  4. Let it sit for 1–2 hours.
  5. Strain or pour off the clearer water from the top.

⚠ Important: This method does not fully purify water on its own.

Whenever possible, boil the water after this process, or use it alongside other purification methods.
